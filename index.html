Bigger, Better, Events

I want to focus on increasing attendance and seeing new faces at every event. What I have heard a lot of is, “I don’t go because none of my friends want to go”. By re-vamping and investing more time into a select 3-4 events that have the most potential, we can draw in more groups of people than who typically attend EngSoc events. To determine which events would be expanded, I will turn to feedback from past events, and ideas from students who rarely attend Engsoc events.

I believe we can get to a point where most engineering undergrads can say “I went to at at least 1 EngSoc event this term”.

Influential Outreach

Many students I have talked to are very, very interested in attending more outreach events and helping out off-campus. I would make Outreach a priority as well. In addition to maintaining the strong outreach traditions with the Society, I will look into finding a new outreach opportunity where we can interact with high school students.

More Inter-Faculty Events

More friends = More fun! I want to hold at least one event per term, joint with another faculty. Inter-faculty events have not been possible in the past due to financial or communication problems.

I propose to make this a priority and get into communication with other faculties’ societies immediately to discuss budgets and ideas.

Chill Ongoing Groups

Some events are great at attracting niche groups of students and providing a space for like-minded people to meet and make friends. I believe that the recurring events in the term should be made to run as weekly/bi-weekly groups with a set meeting time, rather than events that are advertised each week.

Friendly Relations

If I am your VP of Student Life, I promise to be friendly, professional, and diplomatic in all relations with faculty members, engineering students, students of other faculties, and of course all members of the Engsoc Executive team.
